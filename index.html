Snake Thron

You are a mama snake Queen and just layed out your egg in your nest. You need to hurry and take your egg to the thron with you to make it the air to the thron. Protect your egg, be the fastest to take it to the thron and sabetage other potensial airs.

How To Win

The first player that reaches 3 victory point (VP) wins the game.

How do you get VP?

When you reach the thron, for each egg you have on the board you get one VP, and for each time someone else take your egg to the thron you loose one VP.

Starting package:

Each player choose a color and takes the matching:

  • Snake HeadX 1
  • Snake EggX 1
  • Players MatX 1
  • Snake partsX 7
  • Movement CardsX 20

    • The last snake part you have on the bored is your snake tail.
    Snake head, super snake parts and eggs are also count as a snake part.
    All snake parts except the super snake parts gives one requirement point.
    Super snake part gives 3 requirements point.


Set Ups:


Player Set Up:

  1. Place your Snake head outside of the board, next to the arrow tile of your chosen color with the same direction as the arrow.
  2. Place your egg in the nest of your color area.
  3. Place other snake parts in your reserve pool, on your players mat.
  4. Suffle your movement cards, and place them, face down, on the draw part of your players mat.

Board Set Up:

Put these items where everyone can reach it.

  1. Put the Special movement cards, faceup.
  2. Seperate the four type of damage cards (Biten, Poisioned, Hypnotized, Killed), and place them, face up, in four seperate piles.
  3. Seperate the nest and thron upgrade cards, Suffle them and place them, face down, in 2 piles.
  4. Draw three cards from each upgrade pile and place them, face up next to the upgrade piles.
  5. Place the super snake parts ( golden color parts X9 ) in a pile next to the board. These parts are equal to 3 snake parts.

How To Play:


Sneaky Snakes is played in rounds, each round is made up of the following three phases:

    Game phases

  1. Board Preperation Phase
  2. The Stratagy Phase
  3. The Movement Phase

The Board preperation Phase


  1. Roll 2 dices of red and green.
  2. the red one shows the number of the nest/center and the green one shows the number of the tile in that nest/center.
  3. Put one super snake part in this tile.

    4. If a player snake parts is in this tile, super snake parts goes to this player reserve pool and should be used in the next growing move.

    If any of the dices show 0 skip this phase.

    When a player dies, use these steps to determain the respwning tile for that player.

    If you get shot when the super part is in your tail. Put the super snake part back and put 2 normal part in your tail instead.

The Stratagy Phase


All players do this phase simultaneously.

  1. Draw 9 cards from the top of your movement cards deck.
  2. Choose 5 of them and place them, facedown, on your plan slots. ( These 5 cards, show the path you choose to take on the board. )
  3. Put the 4 cards, that you didn't use in your plan slots, in your discard pile.

    What should I do if the movement cards in my draw pile isless than 9 cards?

  1. Keep the ones that are left ( if any )
  2. Shuffle your discard pile, and place it in your draw pile.
  3. Now take as many cards as you need, from the top of the draw deck to reach 9 cards.
  4. Continue as before. ( step 2 from the last list )

The Movement Phase

All players do this phase simultaneously.

This is the main phase of the game. In this phase you use the 5 cards you used on your plan slots to move in the board plan by plan.


In each plan in movement phase do these in this order if some interfiar together:

  1. Move based on priority.
  2. Grow your snake.
  3. Take an egg form the nest
  4. Shoping upgrade cards with your requirement points
  5. Shooting a snake
  6. Bumping to a snake
  7. Take damage cards
  8. take a respawned egg

Move based on priority.


The priority is based on the numbers on the movement cards in that plan slot.
If players have the same priority they should role a dice to determine who moves first.
The highest number moves first and then other players move in priority number.


Grow your snake.


Where and when to place your tail?
  • When there is no empty tile, in 4 directions, around your tail. you can't grow your tail.

    • If this happens when you taking an egg or a super part. These parts go to your reserve and you should put them on the board in your next moves.
    Puting back the egg on the board has the most priority, then a super part and then normal snake parts.

  • When there is only one empty tile, in 4 directions, around your tail. you can grow your tail only in that tile
  • When there is several empty tiles, in 4 directions, around your tail. you can choose which tile you want to plae your tail in.
How can I grow my tail?
  • Grow with snake parts in your reserve pool.
    • when using any forward movement cards.
    • when using a grow up movement cards.
    • When using the again movment card after any of the movement or grow up cards.
  • Grow with taking an egg or a super part on the board

Take an egg from the nest


When you enter a nest, you can take the egg from the nest. ( If it's still there )


Put the egg on your tail if there is empty space to grow, otherwise put it in your reserve pool until you can put it on the board. If you manage to take the egg to the thron when it's in your tail you get one VP point. If you get shot when the egg is in your tail, or someone takes an egg successfully to the thron, the egg spawns back to it's original nest.
If it lands on a snake part. it goes straight to the reserve pool of the snake it landed on.


Upgrade Shops General Rules


Each snake parts, including head and eggs, you have on the board gives you one requirement point and a super snake part gives you 3 requirement points.
you can use these points to take an upgrade card from the shops.
you can have as many upgrade cards you want, as long as their total requirements points is less than or equal to your total requirement points. Otherwise you need to discard as many upgrade cards needed to reach this condition.

There should always be three upgrade cards in each shop. ( 3 red cards and 3 golden cards ).
When a player takes a card from a shop, replace it with another card with the same color imediatly.


You can use your upgrade cards at any time you want.
If there isn't any upgrade card that you like in the nest upgrade shop You may loose a body part on the board to refresh the shop(s). You can't do this if you are not currantly in a shop

The Nest shop:

Nests are marked on the board. When you enter a nest, you can take any minor upgrade cards in the shop.
The number shown on top of each upgrade card is the amount of requirement points they need for you to be able to use it.

Minor upgrade cards ( red color ) are temporary and should be discarded after usage.


The Thron Shop

Thron is placed exactly in the middle tile of the board.
When you are in the thron you can take both major and minor upgrade cards.
Major upgrade cards are permanant but you still need to have the minimum requirment points on the board to keep them, otherwise you should discard the card(s) that passed your requirement point limit.



Shooting a snake


Shots just hit the first snake in their path and don't go through. This counts for any egg or super parts in the way. They would block your shot.

    When a player's snake head, facing any of your snake parts, these situations can happen.

  • If you have a normal snake part in your tail.

    • You get as much damage card as the players damage power. Place the damage cards in your discard pile.
    Players usualy just have one damage power but it can change using an upgrade card.

  • If you have an egg in your tail

    • You get as much damage card as the players damage power. Place the damage cards in your discard pile.
    You also loose the egg. Put it back in it's original nest.


  • If you have a super part in your tail

    • You get as much damage card as the players damage power. Place the damage cards in your discard pile.
    For each damage take one point out from the 3 requirement points of the super part.
    Put the super part back to super parts reserve pool and take back 1 to 2 normal snake parts back to the boar instead, based on the damage of the shooter.
    For example if you took 2 damage, loose the super part and just put one normal part in your tail instead.


Bumping to a snake

    When bumping in to another player's snake, these situations can happen:

  • You bump in to any snake part of another player except head:

    • You die.

    1. Put back all your snake parts from the board to your reserve pool.
    2. Loose the upgrade cards that you can't use.

      3. Since you just respwen with your head you can only have a card that needs 1 requirement point.
    3. Roll a green and red dice to respwen in the tile determined with the dices
      4. ( The same way you determine the placing of a super parts. )
    4. Continue your movements from the spot you respwen.

  • Players move in the same tile together: ( Head bumping )

    • The player with the higher priority moves to this tile and the other player(s) die.


Take Damage Cards

When you get shot by another player you get as much damage cards as that player damage power.
Normally players have 1 attack power but it can increase with some upgrade cards.

Take the damage card and place it in your movement cards discard pile.
Damage cards polute your movement cards deck and limit your movements by poping up in your 9 card choices in stratagy phase.
eventually you might be forced to place them in your plan slots.

  • Biten: loose one snake part from the board
  • poisioned: loose one snake part for each face down movement cards you have in your plan slots. exp: if you use this card in the 3rd plan slot, you should loose 3 parts and if you use the card in the 4rth slot, you loose 2 parts.
  • Hypnotized: Discard all your upgrade cards.
  • Killed instant death, respone and loose all of your upgrades and body parts.

take a respawned egg

When a player get shot, when carring an egg in his/her tail, or a snake takes an egg to the thron successfully, the egg respawn back to it's orginal nest.
If there is a player's snake part in that nest, this player gets the egg in his/her reserve pool and has to put it in his/her tail in the next moves as soon as possible.

Walk through of one movement phase

  1. Turn your movement card, in your first plan slot, faceup.
  2. Each player move based on their priority numbers writen on this card.

    3. The player with highest priority number moves first and then other players move based on their priority.

    If players have the same priority number, role a dice. The higher number determines who goes first.

  3. Each time you move forward, place a snake part on the path you just passed, from your reserve pool.
    4. If you don't have any more parts in your reserve pool, move your tail in the path you just passed, tile by tile
  4. Now do these steps for the rest of the movement cards.
  5. After using all the 5 movement cards on your mat, discard them in your discard pile.
  6. Finish the round and start from the preperation phase again.
